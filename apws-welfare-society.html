Know Your Rights

Information for members of the Puroik community and others who may have faced bonded labour or exploitation.

Your Legal Rights

  • Bonded labour is illegal under the Bonded Labour System (Abolition) Act, 1976.
  • No person can be forced to work to repay a debt.
  • You have the right to freedom, fair wages, and dignity.
  • Children have the right to education and cannot be made to work.

How to Get Help

1. Lodge a Confidential Complaint

Use the "Complaint" button on this website. Your complaint will be seen only by the APWS Executive Committee.

2. Contact Local Authorities

You can also approach the District Magistrate, Police, or Labour Department.

3. Reach Out to APWS

Our team is here to support you with legal guidance and rehabilitation support.

Important Message

If you or someone you know is still facing any form of bonded labour or exploitation, you are not alone. APWS is committed to helping the Puroik community regain their rights and live with dignity. Taking the first step by reporting can bring change not only for you but for many others.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our services and your rights

How do I lodge a complaint?

Click on the red "Complaint" button at the top. Fill the form. Your complaint will only be visible to the APWS Executive Committee.

Is my complaint confidential?

Yes. All complaints are handled confidentially and shared only with the executive committee members.

Who can apply for scholarship?

Students from underprivileged backgrounds, especially from the Puroik community, can apply through the Scholarship Portal.

What if I was forced into bonded labour?

Bonded labour is illegal. You can lodge a confidential complaint using this website or contact APWS directly. We will support you with legal guidance.

How long does it take to get a response?

The committee usually reviews complaints and applications within 7–15 days.
